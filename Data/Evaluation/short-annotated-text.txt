<a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Nicholas_A._Christakis'>Nicholas Christakis</a> explores how the largescale <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Face-to-face_interaction'>face to face</a> <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_network'>social networks</a> in which we are embedded affect our <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Life'>lives</a> and what we can do to take advantage of this <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Fact'>fact</a>. <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/People'>People</a> aren`t merely <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_animal'>social animals</a> in the usual <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Sense'>sense</a> for we don`t just live in <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_group'>groups</a>. We live in <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_network'>networks</a> and we have done so ever since we emerged from the African <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Savanna'>savanna</a>. Via intricately branching paths tracing out cascading <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Family'>family</a> connections, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Interpersonal_ties'>friendship ties</a>, and <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Employment'>work</a> <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Interpersonal_relationship'>relationships</a> we are interconnected to hundreds or thousands of specific people most of whom we do not know. We affect them and they affect us. <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Nicholas_A._Christakis'>Nicholas Christakis</a>` work examines the biological psychological sociological and mathematical <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Norm_%28philosophy%29'>rules</a> that govern how we form these <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_network'>social networks</a> and the <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Norm_%28philosophy%29'>rules</a> that govern how they shape our <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Life'>lives</a>. His work shows how <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Phenomenon'>phenomena</a> as diverse as <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Obesity'>obesity</a>, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Smoking'>smoking</a>, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Emotion'>emotions</a>, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Idea'>ideas</a>, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Microorganism'>germs</a> and <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Altruism'>altruism</a> can spread through our <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Interpersonal_ties'>social ties</a> and how <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Gene'>genes</a> can partially underlie our creation of <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Interpersonal_ties'>social ties</a> to begin with. His work also sheds light on how we might take advantage of an  <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Understanding'>understanding</a> of <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_network'>social networks</a> to make the <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/World'>world</a> a better place. At <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Harvard_University'>Harvard</a>, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Nicholas_A._Christakis'>Christakis</a> is a <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Professor'>Professor</a> of <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Medicine'>Medicine</a>, <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Health_policy'>Health Care Policy</a> and <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Sociology'>Sociology</a> and he directs a diverse <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Research'>research</a> group investigating <a href='http://dbpedia.org/resource/Social_network'>social networks</a>.
